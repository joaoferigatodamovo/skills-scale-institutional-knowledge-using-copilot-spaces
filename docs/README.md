# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management documentation hub! This README provides a single entry point for all project management processes and direct links to detailed guidance on each stage of our delivery lifecycle.

## Project Management Processes Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured, lifecycle-driven approach to project management that emphasizes customer value, iterative delivery, and clear ownership. Projects progress through five distinct phases: **Initiation**, **Planning**, **Execution**, **Release**, and **Close & Retrospective**. Each phase has defined objectives, deliverables, and decision gates to ensure alignment and reduce risk. The methodology prioritizes data-informed decisions, psychological safety, and transparency across all stakeholders, making it suitable for cross-functional teams delivering product features, services, and integrations.

### Key Roles & Communication

Our organization defines three primary personas—**Project Managers** (who coordinate delivery, manage schedules and risks), **Product Managers** (who define outcomes and prioritize the backlog), and **Developers** (who implement features and collaborate on design and quality)—supported by QA/Testing teams and stakeholders. Communication happens through a regular cadence: daily standups focused on progress and blockers, weekly syncs between PM and Product Lead, twice-weekly delivery standups, monthly stakeholder updates, and ad-hoc escalations as needed. This layered communication ensures both day-to-day team alignment and visibility for executive sponsors.

### Execution & Quality Assurance

Execution and quality assurance are managed through a project board workflow (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done) with small, reviewable pull requests (≤400 lines), automated CI/CD testing, linting, and security scanning. Teams maintain a Definition of Done, capture acceptance criteria for each backlog item, and conduct manual QA for feature acceptance when needed. Risk management is formalized through a Risk Register tracking impact, likelihood, mitigation plans, and ownership. Quality gates, pre-release requirements (passing CI, security scans, smoke tests), and a documented rollback playbook ensure safe deployments and rapid incident response.

### Continuous Improvement

Continuous improvement is embedded through scheduled retrospectives after sprints, releases, or milestones, followed by actionable improvements tracked in the project backlog with clear owners and timelines. This holistic approach—combining upfront planning rigor, daily team coordination, structured quality practices, and systematic learning—enables OctoAcme teams to deliver reliably while maintaining alignment with business goals and adapting based on evidence and team feedback.
