# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers conduct user research, design interfaces, and validate usability to ensure features meet user needs and organizational standards. They bridge product vision with user-centered implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design systems
- Collaborate with Product Managers on user requirements
- Review designs during development and provide UI/UX feedback
- Document design decisions and design patterns
- Attend sprint planning and demos to provide design context

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce support burden through clear design
- Ensure consistency across the product

### Typical Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Translates user research and feedback into design requirements
- **With Developers:** Provides design specifications, reviews implementation, iterates on feedback
- **With QA/Testing:** Validates UI/UX acceptance criteria and edge cases
- **With Support/Customer Success:** Gathers feedback on user pain points for future designs

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings and feedback sessions
- Prototype walkthroughs and user testing reports
- Design specifications and component library documentation

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts translate metrics and insights into actionable intelligence. They help teams make data-driven decisions about product performance, user behavior, and project outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track success metrics aligned with project goals
- Create dashboards and reports for visibility into key signals
- Perform root cause analysis on issues or anomalies
- Support retrospectives with performance data and trends
- Provide insights on user adoption, engagement, and retention
- Collaborate on experiment design and A/B testing

### Goals
- Enable data-informed decision making
- Surface trends early to inform course corrections
- Demonstrate project impact to stakeholders

### Typical Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Validates success metrics and provides impact analysis
- **With Project Managers:** Tracks delivery metrics (velocity, quality, time-to-market)
- **With Developers:** Provides performance data and debugging insights
- **With Support/Customer Success:** Analyzes customer feedback patterns and support ticket trends

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics dashboards and trend reports
- Data-driven recommendations in planning and retrospectives
- Deep-dive analysis on critical metrics or anomalies

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile ceremonies, remove team impediments, and foster a culture of continuous improvement. They help teams adhere to Agile principles and adapt processes as needed.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and escalate impediments that teams cannot resolve
- Coach teams on Agile practices and continuous improvement
- Maintain sprint board and burndown health
- Coordinate dependencies across teams
- Support team members with process questions and best practices

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and delivery predictability
- Foster a culture of psychological safety and learning
- Improve iteration cycles and reduce time-to-market

### Typical Interactions
- **With Project Managers:** Coordinates escalation and dependency management; aligns on sprint scheduling
- **With Developers:** Removes technical and organizational blockers
- **With Product Managers:** Ensures backlog is clear and priorities are understood
- **With all team members:** Coaches on Agile ceremony effectiveness

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation
- Retrospective notes and action item tracking
- Sprint metrics and health reports
- Coaching and mentoring conversations

---

## Customer Support Lead / Support Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Support Leads represent the voice of the customer within project planning and execution. They ensure that solutions are supportable, align with customer expectations, and address identified support gaps.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and prioritize customer feedback and support tickets
- Identify recurring issues and escalate to product/engineering
- Participate in sprint planning to flag support-related requirements
- Validate that solutions are adequately documented and discoverable
- Prepare support materials and escalation procedures for new features
- Support incident response and post-mortems

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through proactive issue identification
- Improve customer satisfaction and onboarding experience
- Provide customer perspective to shape product decisions

### Typical Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Escalates customer pain points and suggests feature priorities
- **With Project Managers:** Flags critical customer impacts and mitigation strategies
- **With Developers:** Reviews implementation for supportability and documentation clarity
- **With QA/Testing:** Identifies edge cases based on customer usage patterns
- **With UX Designers:** Feeds customer usability feedback into design iterations

### Typical Communication
- Support ticket summaries and trend reports
- Customer feedback and use-case documentation
- Sprint planning input and feature validation
- Incident escalation and customer communication
